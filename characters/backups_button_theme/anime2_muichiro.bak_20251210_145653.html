Muichirō Tokitō

Biography

Muichirō’s manner is fog-bound: dreamy, distant, and startlingly precise when the moment demands it. He was a prodigious child who rose quickly through mastery yet struggled with emotional anchoring. The story frames him as someone who knows technique deeply but must be nudged back toward connection.

His swordsmanship is like a mist: it obscures intention and then reveals devastating clarity. He fights with a kind of dreamy focus that confounds opponents.

Role in the Story

Muichirō adds a contemplative axis to the cast: he shows how different temperaments can reach similar heights of skill. He forces other characters to rethink assumptions about strength and serenity.

Contribution to Plot

His interventions are often strategic and subtle, providing cover and sudden, decisive strikes in battles when brute force fails.
